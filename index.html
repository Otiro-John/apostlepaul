The Places Reached With
The Gospel Of Christ.

Damascus

Purpose: Conversion & early preaching

Description: On the road to Damascus, Paul encountered the risen Christ, marking the turning point from persecutor to apostle. He immediately began proclaiming Jesus in the synagogues.

Scripture: Acts 9:1–22

Saul, Saul, why are you persecuting me?

Arabia

Purpose: Time of solitude & revelation

Description: After his conversion, Paul withdrew into Arabia for a season of reflection and revelation, receiving the gospel directly from Christ.

Scripture: Galatians 1:17–18

I did not receive it from any man…

Jerusalem

Purpose: Reporting & unity

Description: Paul visited the apostles to report his calling and seek unity between Jewish and Gentile believers. He also defended the gospel during the Jerusalem Council.

Scripture: Acts 9:26–30; Acts 15:1–35

They gave me the right hand of fellowship…

Antioch

Purpose: Early discipleship & base of missions

Description: Paul taught in Antioch with Barnabas, and it became the base for his missionary journeys.

Scripture: Acts 11:25–26

In Antioch the disciples were first called Christians.

Ephesus

Purpose: Teaching & confronting false religion

Description: Paul spent significant time in Ephesus, teaching daily and confronting the worship of Artemis, leading to revival and opposition.

Scripture: Acts 19:1–41

Great is Artemis of the Ephesians!

Rome

Purpose: Final ministry & house arrest

Description: Paul appealed to Caesar and was taken to Rome, where he continued teaching while under house arrest, writing key epistles.

Scripture: Acts 28:16–31

Boldly and without hindrance he preached…

Meet The Apostle

Apostle Paul

Apostle Paul, once Saul of Tarsus, was transformed by an encounter with Jesus Christ and became one of the most influential voices in the early Church. Through his letters and journeys, he proclaimed the gospel with clarity and boldness, leaving a legacy of faith, grace, and devotion that continues to shape the Church today.
